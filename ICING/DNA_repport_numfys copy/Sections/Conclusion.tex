\section{Conclusion}
% oppsummerer resultatene og setter de inn i den faglige samtalen

A clear transition from discrete to continuous magnetic domains was observed for nanomagnetic polygons, where polygons with fewer sides exhibited more discrete magnetic domains, while polygons with more sides showed a more continuous rotation %of the magnetization vectors around the center of each structure
leading to an overall lower average curl across the structure. The relationship was found for polygons with a radius of approximately $900$, $600$ and $400$ nm. Our results also demonstrate how geometric factors influence the transition from discrete magnetic domains to continuous vortices. This substantiates the claim of vortex formation being a geometry-driven phenomenon \cite{behncke2018_magnetic_vortices}, consistent with previous findings and theoretical expectations.
